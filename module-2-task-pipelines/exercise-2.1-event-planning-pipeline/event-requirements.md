# Annual Company Summit: Event Brief

**From**: Priya Sharma, Head of Operations
**To**: Event Planning Team
**Date**: February 2026
**Event Date**: April 18, 2026

## Event Overview

Our annual company summit brings together employees, key clients, and industry partners for a day of presentations, networking, and team building. This year's theme is "Building Tomorrow Together."

## Requirements

### Capacity

- 80 confirmed guests (see guest-list.csv for details)
- Need seating for keynote (theater style, 80 seats)
- Need seating for lunch (round tables, 10 per table)
- Need 3 breakout rooms for afternoon workshops (20 people each)

### Catering

- Morning coffee/pastry reception (7:30-8:30 AM)
- Plated lunch (12:00-1:00 PM)
- Afternoon snack break (3:00-3:30 PM)
- Cocktail reception (5:00-6:30 PM)
- MUST accommodate: 8 vegetarian, 4 vegan, 3 gluten-free, 2 halal, 1 kosher (see guest list)

### Technical

- Main stage: projector, screen, 2 wireless mics, laptop connection
- 3 breakout rooms: each needs a screen and whiteboard
- WiFi must support 80 simultaneous devices
- Live stream capability for 20 remote attendees

### Accessibility

- Wheelchair accessible venue (2 guests require wheelchair access)
- Hearing loop or caption service (1 guest is hearing impaired)
- All materials in large print available on request

### Budget

- Total budget allocated: See budget.csv
- Budget is FIRM -- no overruns without VP approval
- Need 10% contingency reserve

### Timeline

- Venue must be booked by March 1 (6 weeks out)
- Catering menu finalized by March 15
- Guest confirmations by April 1
- Final logistics plan by April 10

## What Success Looks Like

- Every guest's dietary and accessibility needs are met
- Technology works flawlessly during presentations
- Under budget with contingency preserved
- Guests rate the event 4+ out of 5 in post-event survey

## What I Do NOT Want

- A generic hotel conference room with bad lighting
- "We'll figure out the AV on the day" -- test everything in advance
- Guests discovering their dietary needs were ignored at lunch
