# Competitor Profile: Pet Palace

## Overview

- **Founded**: 1995 (as physical retail chain)
- **Headquarters**: Chicago, IL
- **Revenue**: ~$850M total (estimated $45M from online/delivery)
- **Employees**: 4,200 (180 in digital division)
- **Stores**: 320 locations across 28 states

## Products & Services

- Full range of pet food (economy to ultra-premium)
- Same-day delivery from local stores in 85 metro areas
- In-store pickup within 2 hours
- Pet grooming, vet clinics, and training classes in stores
- Loyalty program with 2.8M members

## Strengths

- Massive physical footprint enables same-day delivery
- Trusted brand with 30 years of history
- Can bundle food delivery with services (grooming, vet visits)
- Enormous purchasing power -- best wholesale pricing in the industry
- Loyalty program data on millions of pet owners' buying habits

## Weaknesses

- Digital experience is an afterthought (app rated 2.8 stars)
- Online team is separate from retail, causing inventory conflicts
- Premium online delivery competes with their own stores (channel conflict)
- Subscription model is basic (fixed monthly shipment, no customization)
- No fresh/refrigerated delivery capability
- Slow to innovate -- still running 2019-era e-commerce platform

## Pricing

- Standard delivery: $5.99 flat rate (free over $75)
- Same-day delivery: $12.99
- Subscription discount: 10% off recurring orders
- Average online order value: $62

## Recent Moves

- Hired VP of Digital Transformation in November 2025
- Piloting "Pet Palace Express" delivery-only micro-fulfillment centers
- Testing premium subscription tier with personalized nutrition advice
- Acquired a small pet food AI startup (unannounced, rumored at $8M)
